# PHACT Lab

**Public Health policy Analysis, Causal inference & Translation Lab**
🎓 University of California, Los Angeles (UCLA); New York University (NYU)

---

## 📖 About

The PHACT Lab studies how policies, interventions, and exposures shape population health. We use rigorous **causal inference** methods to move beyond correlation and estimate the real effects of public health decisions and we work to **translate** those findings into evidence that policymakers, practitioners, and communities can act on.

Our work sits at the intersection of biostatistics, health policy, community health sciences and social sciences. The through-line is a commitment to asking causal questions carefully and answering them with methods matched to the data and the decision at hand (hence this repository where all the relevant codes will be open-source).

## 🔬 What we do

- 📊 **Policy analysis**: Evaluating the health effects of laws, regulations, and programs (e.g., natural experiments created by policy changes).
- 🎯 **Causal inference**: Applying and developing methods for estimating causal effects from observational and quasi-experimental data using modern machine-learning-based estimators.
- 🌍 **Exposure and intervention assessment**: Quantifying the population health impact of environmental, social, and behavioral exposures.
- 🗣️ **Translation**: Turning findings into accessible evidence for policymakers, public health agencies, and the public.
